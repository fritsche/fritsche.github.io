Education

(Click on the item to expand it.)

Gian is a Ph.D. in the field of machine learning and evolutionary computation. In his work, Gian investigated and proposed machine learning techniques for the automatic selection of heuristics, known as hyperheuristics. In his work, heuristics are multi-objective evolutionary algorithms used to optimize problems with multiple competing criteria. The results of his work were published in national and international conferences of relevance in the area. Gian studied data science techniques, machine learning, evolutionary computation, and parallel computing during his doctorate period.

In his master's degree, Gian studied automatic selection techniques (hyper-heuristics) of methods that compose the multi-objective particle swarm optimization algorithm. His studies included hyperheuristics for software engineering problems, such as integration and test order problems, and evaluating problem benchmarks from the literature.

Gian studied Desktop, Mobile, and web development techniques in his education in Systems Analysis and Development. In addition to design patterns and network and database technologies. Among the tools used were C, Java, and SQL. In his conclusion work, Gian studied robot soccer in a simulated environment.

Sandwich graduated from the Science without Borders program, in which he studied optimization methods, robotics, and swarm intelligence.
